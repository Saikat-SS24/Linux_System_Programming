The Linux operating system originated in 1991 when Linus Torvalds, a Finnish student, developed a free Unix-like 
kernel as a hobby project and released its source code publicly. At the same time , Richard Stallman's GNU Project
, started in 1983, had already created most essential components of a free operating system, including compilers,
utilities and libraries but lacked a kernel. The combination of the Linux with a GNU tools formed the complelete
GNU/Linux system.
GNU/Linux is based on the principles of the free software, emphasizing users freedom to run, study, modify and 
didtribute the software. It follows the Unix philosophy of modular, simple tools and promotes open collaboration,
where contributions are accepted based on merit. This model has some GNU/Linux highly portable, stable and widely
used across servers, devices and dekstops.
